WHO AM I?


Trainer at the EI CESI After working as an engineer in the field for almost 15 years, I was drawn to the need to pass on knowledge and skills to the teams within the company I worked for. So I became an industrial energy trainer, a job I've been doing for 5 years now.

I wanted to develop strong skills in pedagogical engineering so as to offer quality training courses. Because it's all very well being an expert in the field you're teaching, but it's even better if you know how to teach it using the right pedagogical techniques.

So I trained in pedagogical engineering, then in multimedia pedagogical engineering to keep up to date, and finally I trained as a graphic designer to be complete in my work as a pedagogical designer.
In the course of my professional experiences , I have been able to develop skills in:

  • Project Management;
  • Industrial energy trainer;
  • Graphic Design.

PROJECT#1


♦ The context of the project

As part of my work at Fives, I developed a number of training courses enabling me to modify equipment, including spin-dryers. These centrifuges are used to obtain crystal sugar.


♦ Implementation phase

The first stage consisted of a theoretical study, followed by an on-site performance survey.
Various proposals were then made to create a training program to modify the design of the machines used.


♦ Achievements

The new skills acquired by the teams enabled them to increase the speed of rotation and modify the geometry of the baskets, thereby increasing the performance of the machines.

PROJECT#2


♦ The context of the project

As part of my work at NEU, I was asked to develop a blended-learning course on the design of a pneumatic powder conveying test loop. The aim of this training was to enable employees to make this loop a showcase for customers, as well as a means of studying pneumatic conveying parameters.


♦Implementation phase

The development of the blended-learning course took 1 year of work, working alone on the different phases of the ADDIE Model, with two experts to review the content I had developed before and after implementation on the training platform.


♦ Achievements

Numerous tests were carried out on the transport loop, enabling us to optimize transport parameters, in particular to reduce energy consumption.

WHO AM I?


As a former training project managerstrong> in a number of companies, most recently at the Luxembourg Lifelong Learning Centre, I have always pursued my professional development in the field of vocational training, a sector in the throes of change, with a new relationship to knowledge linked to the use of new technologies.
It's a challenge for today's society that I've chosen to take up in my own way.
To meet this challenge, I specialized in project management techniques (8 years), and in particular in the search for effective solutions for transmitting and sharing knowledge in a personalized pedagogical approach.
The know-how I've developed over the course of my career enables me to be operational in the following tasks:

  • Designing, implementing and managing training projects;
  • Supervising and monitoring teams of trainers;
  • Facilitating interactive and creative teaching methods.

I've also acquired knowledge in instructional design and have a predilection for the digital training sector, in which I completed a distance learning Master's degree in Ingénierie Pédagogique Multimédia to become a blended-learning project manager.
Focusing on the development of training courses in UX Design mode, I design and produce blended-learning programs in line with customer needs, deadlines and budget constraints.

PROJECT#1


♦ The context of the project

APapp has applied for ESF funding to provide trainers from the APP network with training and practical tools for applying the Apprendre à apprendrestrong> method within their respective structures.


♦ Implementation phase

I coordinated, piloted and operationalized the project European Key Skills developing tools for program management, organization and information processing. I also worked as part of a team to develop a digital CléA training course.

PROJECT#2


♦ The context of the project

Staff from the Big Four (Deloitte, EY, PWC and KPMG) organized face-to-face courses at LLLC. The time they spent on courses was lost on the projects they had to carry out for their clients. So they asked us to offer digital training courses to make up for this loss of time.


♦ Implementation phase

Working in collaboration with external service providers, we were able to develop a blended-learning course that would reduce the amount of face-to-face training time for trainers, while optimizing learner learning, thanks to the ADDIE model and the use of engaging and efficient UX design techniques.


♦ Achievements

Following design and development, including evaluation phases, it was possible to launch the first "Individual Income Tax" blended-learning course for the first cohort of learners. The other 3 courses were subsequently successfully rolled out.
